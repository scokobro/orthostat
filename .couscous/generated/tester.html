Intro

media and cultural studies grew up in the era of the press, broadcasting and mass consumption

readers/viewers were after entertainment and information

cultural studies academics saw danger - political and commercial exploitation

C and MS 'founded in suspicion of owners and controllers' - mass media is a problem

"sender -> message -> receiver"

There is room for duplicity and deceit at every link: producers (hidden) motives? a message with hidden meanings? receivers doing things they would otherwise not have done? (their 'true' self has been changed - their real behaviour has been influenced)

Digital - interactive - participatory media: not receivers but USERS.

meaningfulness social networks relationships

emphasis on 'content/info' replace by human interaction based on self-expression, description and argumentation, also 'phatic' chatter and imaginative invention

Needs a different approach to the ideas of duplicity, 'hidden purposes', exploitation
